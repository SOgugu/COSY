College students are experiencing all-time high rates of depression, anxiety and suicidality (Healthy Minds Survey, 2023). The onset of a majority of mental illnesses takes place in adolescence and early adulthood, and students in their first year of college are particularly susceptible to struggling with their mental health. Research from different countries show a high frequency of mental health problems among college students in their fist year. Until now, there have been no conclusive reports on the mental health of first year students in Germany. It has been shown that mental healthproblems negatively affect academic functioning over thesubsequent years [4, 6]. Furthermore, a reduction in help-seeking behavior has been reported. Several authorshave highlighted that stigmatizing attitudes are a barrier tohelp-seeking behavior in students
